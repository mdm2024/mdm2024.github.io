KEYNOTE 2


Trajectory management and exploration

- Baihua Zheng , Singapore Management University (SMU), Singapore

Abstract

The growing significance of location-based services and the wide availability of trajectory data have garnered considerable interest in trajectory management and exploration. Trajectory management pertains to the effective storage, retrieval, and processing of trajectories, while trajectory exploration focuses on analysing and extracting insights from data representing the movement of objects or entities over time. In this keynote address, we will present our contributions to trajectory management and exploration across four distinct categories: trajectory volume, trajectory variety, trajectory variability, and trajectory value. Our work provides effective strategies for handling large trajectory datasets, discovering diverse movement patterns, unravelling spatiotemporal correlations, and unveiling the untapped value inherent in trajectory data.

Bio

Prof. Baihua Zheng received her BSc degree in Computer Science and Engineering from Zhejiang University in 1999 and the PhD degree in Computer Science from Hong Kong University of Science & Technology in 2003. She is currently a Professor in the School of Computing and Information Systems, Singapore Management University. Her research interests include mobile/pervasive computing, spatial databases, and big data analytics. Prof. Zheng has published more than 160 research papers in refereed journals and conferences including SIGMOD, VLDB, KDD, SIGIR, AAAI, IJCAI, Ubicomp, ICDE, TODS, TKDE, and VLDBJ.
